## Introduction

An essential part of interoperability lies in a common understanding of the meaning of exchanged data between the parties involved. Standards that have strong semantics, e.g. standards that mandates clearf description of the objects in the message, are preferred. Ideally, you would want to use one central vocabulary where are defintions reside. However, standards are built around certain domains and thereby cover a limited area.
